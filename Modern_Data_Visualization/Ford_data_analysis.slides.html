Investigation Overview¶

The aim of this presentation is to extract insights that will help the company to better exploit the resources and prioritize its segmentations and users by distinguishing the dominant behavior of different users.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset is extracted from the publically-published FORD-GOBIKE dataset. This Analysis focuses on the first 6 months of the year 2018.

How much distance are the bikes often used for?¶

The upcoming graph shows the distribution of the distances traveled by the members. and the following could be concluded:

  1. Most renters use the bikes for relatively short distances (< 2km).
  2. There are very few bikers who are traveling long distances, this shows that the bikes, overall, are just used for minor purposes, probably just commuting to work or school.
    So, companies shouldn't focus their production lines on highly sophisticated bikes, rather, medium and adequate products.

Which category of members is the most distinguished?¶

Another interesting information that the data suggests also is:

  1. Male Members are much more than Females and other gender Members.
  2. Dominant year of birth $\approx$ 1981 -> 1995, which defines an age range of 23 -> 31. Most of who are graduates and employees.
  3. The Least remarked users are thoes older than 31 year and belong other genders.

Deeper into the Gender vs. Age Relation:¶

  • A more tangible relation could be detected in the line plot graph, which emphasizes that the age range observed previously is a common range for all genders (Males, Female, and Other). which provide a clear segmentation target for the company to concentrate on this age category (23 to 31 year old).

How much time does different users usually use the bike for?¶

  • It looks like the customers are renting bikes more than subscribers, which indicates that the pay-as-you-go stategy is more preferable than the subsciption packages.
  • Secondly, In the customers, Females are spending more time which contradictes The Previous observation, and the only explanation is that females are a bit slower than other genders when driving a bike.

Does the usage varies over time for the customers?¶

  • Apparently, There was a strong relation between the duration, distance and the user type. Customer-type users are the dominant users; they have the highest duration and distances in all categories.

  • Even when viewing each month seperately, the customer-type users are still the highest in the distance traveled.

    This should provide a hint for the company to elevate the offers in order to attract more subscribers, given that this is the company's vision.

Conclusion

  • Most members are Males and age from 23 to 31 years old, therefore, the company should consider these categories the most while providing the service in the future.
  • Customers are the dominant members in the bikes' usage. A proper strategy should be initiated in order to account for this section of users.
  • Females are the slowest bikers, a more efficient bikes should be provided t the females in order to overcome this gap in distance vs. time between the genders.
